Starting Out

2020-04-08

I've had this website for a few years now, but I haven't really done anything with it. I've changed the design a dozen times and rewritten it half a dozen times, but there hasn't been any consistent content. At this point, it just serves as a nearly empty portfolio. I want that to change, though I'm not yet sure what it'll look like in practice.

So... what are the goals for the writing on my website? Four quick, fairly unrefined thoughts:

Sharing What I Learn

Sharing what I learn is helpful. It will organize my thoughts on a subject, aid someone else in learning something new, etc. However, I want to not only share what I learn, but use writing about a topic as the end goal, to help me learn more. Do I want to learn how to build a certain piece of software? Great! The fun is in the process itself and getting the end product... but writing about it could be my own way of "completing" what I set out to learn. The wax stamp sealing the letter.

There are a lot of great writers out there that do this (these are not new concepts). Julia Evans' website is a great example. Her blog's theme was once described as "things Julia has learned recently." She shares what she learns, and it's been helpful enough that she has a meta section titled "On blogging / speaking."

Becoming a Better Writer

Writing more will help me become a better writer. (That's basically the tl;dr of all "become a better writer!" books.) Having someone read and give constructive feedback would probably be the second best thing.

Content & Quality Goals

I want to write about two main concepts I am passionate about: spirituality and programming.

"Spirituality" is fairly vague. Mostly, I'd like to write about what I learn from reading the Bible and other Bible based books. There are many other topics this could branch to though.

Programming is what I currently do as a full time job. Writing about it may look like anything from in-depth code discussion, to the importance of having mentors.

For both of these topics, I want to write with quality. I'm not a great writer, so in a sense, the quality of my writing is directly proportional to the time I put in. The more time I work on a piece, the better it will be, and the less time I put in, the worse it will be. I'd like to shoot for somewhere in the middle; quality content that I can get out in a few hours. As time goes on, hopefully my time put in will produce a higher value than before.

Paul Graham's essays are very polished and flow well.

Getting Good Jobs

This doesn't need any explanation. Learning, being a better writer, having focused content and high quality are all important. If I can, I'd hope this all assists me whenever I'm looking for new work.

So... what's next?

When you really want to get something done, you should make something akin to SMART goals. I won't do that yet. For now, I'm living life one day at a time.
